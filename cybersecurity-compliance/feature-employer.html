Business Model · B2B

🏢 Sold as an Employer Benefit

The app is offered to staff as a health perk, and the employer's HR receives data on employees. The legal risk lives in that hand-off: what crosses from the app to HR, and on what basis.

👥
Employees
Data subjects
Use the app as individuals
Raw health & wellness data
With explicit consent
📱
Life Coach AI
Processor
Holds raw data, builds the aggregate
Aggregated statistics only
No re-identification
🏢
Employer HR
Recipient
Sees team-level trends, never individuals
⚠️ The consent trap In an employment relationship there's a power imbalance, so regulators say employee "consent" is rarely freely given. You usually can't lean on consent as your legal basis, and HR must never be able to single out an individual from the aggregates.
① What the Law Expects
