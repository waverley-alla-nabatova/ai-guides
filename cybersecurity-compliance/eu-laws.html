European Union · Poland Pilot

How EU Law Stacks Up

In the EU, the framework is harmonised: a single body of regulations and directives applies across all member states. Launching in Poland means complying with EU-wide law first; Polish national rules sit on top of it.

European Union · Cross-Border Framework
EU · GDPR
General Data Protection Regulation
What it protectsPeople's personal data: any information that can identify someone, like names, emails, location, or health details.
What's needed
  • Keep a record of what personal data you collect and why
  • Assess the privacy risks before launching anything sensitive
  • Report a data breach to the regulator within 72 hours
  • Build privacy into the product from the start, not bolt it on later
  • Sign a data-protection agreement with every vendor that touches the data
European Union · Cross-Border Framework
EU · NIS2
Network & Information Security Directive
What it protectsThe security and uptime of important digital services and the networks they run on.
What's needed
  • Have clear policies for managing cybersecurity risks
  • Report serious incidents fast: first alert in 24h, details in 72h, full report later
  • Company leadership is personally accountable if security fails
European Union · Cross-Border Framework
EU · CRA
Cyber Resilience Act
What it protectsUsers of any product with software in it, making sure what they buy is secure, not full of holes.
What's needed
  • Design products to be secure from the start
  • Keep a list of every software component you ship, so you know what's inside
  • Have a clear way to receive and fix reported security flaws
European Union · Cross-Border Framework
EU · AI Act
Artificial Intelligence Act
What it protectsPeople from harm caused by AI systems, especially "high-risk" ones that affect health, money, or rights.
What's needed
  • Check how a high-risk AI system could affect people's rights before launch
  • Make sure the AI is secure and hard to manipulate
European Union · Cross-Border Framework
EU · DORA
Digital Operational Resilience Act
What it protectsThe financial system, keeping banks and fintechs running even when their IT or cloud providers fail or get attacked.
What's needed
  • Keep a register of all your IT and cloud vendors
  • Report major IT incidents to the regulator
  • Mainly applies if you operate in the financial sector
European Union · Cross-Border Framework
EU · Cybersecurity Act
EU Cybersecurity Act
What it protectsThe whole EU, by setting common cybersecurity standards so products can prove they're secure.
What's needed
  • Backs ENISA, the EU's cybersecurity agency, to set the rules
  • Lets products earn an EU-wide cybersecurity certificate
Poland · National Law (out of scope today) On top of the EU framework, Poland has its own national legislation, including local employment-law rules that bear directly on the employer-benefit model (how employee health data may be used, and what HR can receive). We won't cover Polish local legislation in this talk, just flag that it exists and adds a further layer to anything launched in Poland.
