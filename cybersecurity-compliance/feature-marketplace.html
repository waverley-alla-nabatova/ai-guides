Business Model · Marketplace

🏋️ Gym & Trainer Marketplace

The app studies what the user's data reveals (fitness level, habits, health) to recommend the right gyms, individual trainers, and specific exercises. That layers two risks: it profiles the user on sensitive data, and to connect them it shares data with third parties it doesn't control.

⚠️ You're responsible for who you share with Once data reaches a third-party trainer, you don't control how they use it, but you're still on the hook for the hand-off. Share too much, or with an unvetted partner, and the liability is yours.
① What the Law Expects
EU · GDPR + AI Act
Recommending from health data is profiling on sensitive data: needs explicit consent + transparency
EU · GDPR
Each gym/trainer is a separate controller: needs a data contract
EU · GDPR
Share only the minimum data needed for the booking
California · CCPA / CPRA
Sharing counts as a "sale" unless contractually limited to the service
US · FTC
You're accountable for what partners do with shared data
