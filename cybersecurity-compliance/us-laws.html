United States · California Pilot

How US Law Stacks Up

The US has no single federal privacy or cybersecurity law. Obligations come from two directions: sector-specific rules tied to the type of data you hold, and the state law of wherever your users are. For a California pilot, both apply at once.

California · State Law
California · CCPA / CPRA
Consumer Privacy Act (as amended)
Applies toFor-profit businesses meeting CCPA thresholds that handle California residents' data
Key requirementsHealth, geolocation & financial data are Sensitive PI: opt-in & right to limit use; access, deletion & correction rights; "reasonable security"
EnforcementCA Privacy Protection Agency & Attorney General; statutory fines per violation; limited private right of action for breaches
California · State Law
California · CMIA
Confidentiality of Medical Information Act
Applies toAnyone handling Californians' medical information
Key requirementsNo disclosure of medical information without the individual's written authorization
EnforcementPrivate right of action: individuals sue directly, per affected person; statutory damages, no regulator needed
California · State Law
California · Breach Law
Data Breach Notification (Civ. Code §1798.82)
Applies toAny business holding California residents' personal information
Key requirementsNotify affected residents without unreasonable delay after a breach; notify the AG above 500 residents
EnforcementAG enforcement plus a private right of action for failure to notify
California · State Law
California · SB-327
IoT Security Law
Applies toManufacturers of connected devices sold in California
Key requirementsShip with "reasonable" security features (e.g. unique pre-programmed passwords), relevant for paired wearables & fitness hardware
EnforcementEnforced by the California Attorney General
